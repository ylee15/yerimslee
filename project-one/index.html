Avant Garde is a geometrically shaped typeface with large circular counters, straight lines, and a tall X-height. The typeface was created as a display face meant to be tightly kerned and closely leaded. Lubalin was inspired by the sans serif typeface Futura as well as the 1920s Bauhaus movement when he designed the original Avant Garde logo. The type for the Avant Garde logo was originally all caps with uniquely fitted character placements (ligatures).
